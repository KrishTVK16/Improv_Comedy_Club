5 Ways to Conquer Stage Fright Before Your First Open Mic

By Sarah Jenkins
Senior Instructor

Everyone gets butterflies. The trick isn't to kill them, but to make them fly in formation. Whether you're a seasoned pro or stepping onto the stage for the very first time, the adrenaline rush is real.

Here are five battle-tested strategies to keep your cool when the spotlight hits:

1. Breathe (Seriously)

It sounds simple, but it's the first thing we forget to do. Take three deep breaths into your diaphragm before you walk on stage. It signals to your nervous system that you are safe.

2. Start with the Truth

In improv, we say "truth is funny." If you are nervous, admit it. Characters who are nervous are relatable. Trying to hide it makes you look stiff.

"The audience wants you to succeed. They are on your side from the moment you take the mic."

3. Focus on Your Partner

If you're doing improv, get out of your head and into your partner's eyes. Make them look good. When you focus on serving the scene, your ego (and fear) takes a back seat.
